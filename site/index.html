The blind spot in every AI

Every model remembers something. None of them remember your reasoning.

Each tool ships its own kind of memory, and every one of them drops the part that actually matters the moment a chat ends or you change tools. Here is what breaks, tool by tool, and what Continuum does about it.

Claudeauto summary
The gap. When the window fills, Claude compresses your chat into a summary you never see and cannot steer. It stays locked inside Claude.
With Continuum. You choose what gets saved, you can read every word of it, and it follows you to any other AI.
ChatGPTmemory
The gap. Memory stores facts about you, not the state of your work. It knows your name and forgets your architecture.
With Continuum. The working state of every decision is captured and resumes anywhere, not just inside one product.
Grokchat export
The gap. Export hands you a one way wall of text. No decisions, no rejected options, and no way to import it back and continue.
With Continuum. A structured, two way resume package that picks the work back up in Grok or anywhere else.
Codex, Claude Codesession file
The gap. Sessions sit on disk, but every new one starts cold, and nothing carries the reasoning to another tool.
With Continuum. It reads the session file itself, extracts the reasoning, and autosave keeps every single turn.
Geminichat only
The gap. Context lives and dies with the conversation. A decision made here cannot travel to another provider.
With Continuum. A paste ready resume that works everywhere, plus a hosted connector where the platform supports one.
All of themacross months
The gap. Not one of them can hand back your work on a subject from six weeks ago, or the reason you ruled an option out.
With Continuum. Recall gathers every checkpoint on a subject across months of history, with the why intact.

Facts are cheap to recall. Reasoning is what dies.

A fact you lose can be looked up again. The reasoning behind a decision cannot. Once it is gone, you pay for it twice: once to rebuild it, and once in the mistakes you repeat.

Questions, answered

Everything people ask before they connect it.

Do I need an API key or an account to try it?
No. The free tier runs entirely on your machine with zero keys and zero services. Install it, save a chat, resume it. Keys only enter the picture if you want cleaner LLM extraction or the hosted knowledge graph.
What exactly does Continuum store?
Two things, both readable by you: the verbatim conversation as the source of truth, and a structured reasoning layer on top, meaning the decisions, the reasons, the rejected options, and the open questions. Nothing is hidden and everything exports.
How is this different from ChatGPT memory or Claude projects?
Those remember things inside one product. Continuum remembers the working state of your thinking and moves it between products. Save in Claude, continue in Grok, finish in Codex. No built in memory crosses that line.
Will a long project eventually overflow the context window?
No. Resume packages are bounded by a token budget, digest compresses older history while keeping recent work in full, and recall pulls only the subject you ask for. A year of work still arrives as one clean paste.
What happens to my data if I cancel?
You keep everything. Export any project as Markdown or JSON at any moment, and the free local tier keeps working forever. Leaving takes one command, which is exactly how it should be.
Can my whole team share one memory?
Yes. The Team tier gives every person an isolated memory plus shared projects, so anyone can see what was decided and why without digging through chat logs.
